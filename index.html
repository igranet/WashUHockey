Ice Hockey

About The Team ​ ​
​ ​ The Washington University ice hockey team was founded in 2014 and currently plays in Division III of the American Collegiate Hockey Association.
This season the WashU Bears succeeded in becoming MACHA regular and post season champions. Over the course of 2 playoff games the Bears scored 24 goals and only allowed one. ​
The team practices 3 times a week and plays two games each week against teams like Ole Miss and Missouri State. Home games are played in the former St. Louis Blues practice arena, the modern and beautiful "Mills."
​ There are 24 players currently on the team (including goalies), and 2 Coaches.
​ We are also extremely proud of our close community ties, often scrimmaging against the St. Louis responders, a hockey team composed of St. Louis's finest police officers and firefighters.

About The University ​
Washington University in St. Louis is a private institution that was founded in 1853, located in scenic St. Louis. It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 7,675, its setting is suburban, and the campus size is 169 acres. Wash U is divided into four schools that serve undergraduate and graduate students, and four graduate schools.
​ Among Wash U’s graduate programs are the top-ranked George Warren Brown School of Social Work, the highly ranked Olin Business School, School of Law, School of Medicine, Sam Fox School of Design and Visual Arts, School of Engineering and Applied Science and a graduate program in education. ​
Washington University's undergraduate program is ranked 18th by U.S. News & World Report in 2018 and 11th by The Wall Street Journal in their 2018 rankings. The university is ranked 20th in the world in 2017 by the Academic Ranking of World Universities. ​
WashU is also notable for being consistently ranked number 1 in the nation for housing, food, and campus beauty. ​
The acceptance rate for the class of 2022 (those entering in the fall of 2018) was 15%, with students selected from more than 31,000 applications. Of students admitted 81 percent were in the top 10 percent of their class.
